Concept and Culture

Software engineers should take their promises of software quality as a part of their culture.

The healthy software engineering culture includes many features and the understanding that trade-offs among cost, schedule, and quality are a basic tenant of the engineering of any product.

A strong software engineering ethic assumes that engineers accurately report information, conditions, and outcomes related to quality.

Ethics also play a significant role in software quality, the culture, and the attitudes of software engineers.

Why we care

First, a quality culture cannot be bought. Quality culture cannot be bolted on to an organization, it has to be designed-in and nurtured.

The second reason, any change an organization wants to make, cannot simply be ordered; the organization has to cope with the current culture when making a change in maturity, especially when such a change implies a profound change in that culture.

Facts have proved that culture not only can drive reforms but also can prevent reform.

What is Quality

Quality is conformance to requirements, both explicit and implicit.

– meeting the requirements,

– generating customer/stakeholder satisfaction.

  • a key factor in the long term success

  • doesn’t appear by chance

  • requires on ongoing commitment by all parties

Software Quality Assurance

Software quality assurance (SQA) is a process which assures that all software engineering processes, methods, activities and work items are monitored and comply against the defined standards.

The Objectives of SQA Activities

  • Ensuring, with acceptable levels of confidence, conformance to functional technical requirements.
  • Ensuring, with acceptable levels of confidence, conformance to managerial requirements of scheduling and budgets.
  • Initiating and managing activities for the improvement and greater efficiency of software development and SQA activities.

Software Errors, Software Faults and Software Failures

Software errors are sections of the code that are partially or totally incorrect as a result of a grammatical, logical, or other type of mistake made by a system analyst, programmer, or other member of the software development team. (Artificial)

Software faults are software errors that cause the incorrect functioning of the software during one of its specific applications. (Non-artificial)

Software faults become software failures only when they are “activated”.

The root of any software failure is a software error.

Reason

  1. Faulty requirements definition (the most expensive)
  2. Client–developer communication failures
  3. Deliberate deviations from software requirements
  4. Logical design errors (the cheapest)
  5. Coding errors
  6. Noncompliance with documentation and coding instructions
  7. Shortcomings of the testing process
  8. User interface and procedure errors
  9. Documentation errors

The costs and impacts of bad quality

The cost of poor quality comprises not only the costs resulting from product defects, but also company processes, practices, or functions that generate defects and errors.
Poor quality can also weaken consumer relationships, damage your brand, and add major operational and financial costs.

Poor software development or design:
Limits company growth
Badly impacts staff productivity
Destroy the image of the company in the market
