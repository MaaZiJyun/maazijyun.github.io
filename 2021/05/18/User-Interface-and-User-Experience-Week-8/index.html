User Interface and User Experience Week 8
2021-05-18

Today’s class our group leader, Aous, presented our group project process.

The information about the interview:

Technique used:

Research users’ background.

Engage in an interview using google form.

Use the answers as data research for requirements of our tool.

Target user:

Web developers

Web designers

Beginner with Prototyping

Students

Graphic designers

Art industry practitioner

Interview Scheme:

  1. What position/experience level are you in web development?
  2. What do you think of the concept of COLOR IT?
  3. What problems have you met when designing with colours?
  4. What is your biggest design challenge about using colours?
  5. When do you think you are more likely to face the problems of colour design?
  6. In what way do they show you the notes or teaching material?
  7. Can you tell us what you like and dislike about our tool?
  8. Confused when using colours, did you realize that you need to use the appropriate program/website?
  9. What tools have you used to help you solve colour design problems?
  10. Based on your answer from the previous question. Did you find these tools helpful?
  11. What do you think are the shortcomings of those tools?
  12. Have you heard about COLOR IT?
  13. What kind of comment do you hear about this tool?(who wrote this)
  14. In your view, do you think tools like COLOR IT can help in your future career?

User Journey Map

What is UJM?

It is a visualization of the process that a person (series of user actions, thoughts and emotions) goes through in order to accomplish a goa.

Key components of UJM

  • Actor/ Persona
  • Scenario and Expectations
  • Journey Phases
  • Actions, Mindsets, and Emotions
  • Opportunities

Why we use UJM?

  • the process of making a map forces conversation and an aligned mental model for the whole team.
  • the shared artefact resulting from the mapping can be used to communicate an understanding of your user or service to all involved

Benefits of UJM

  1. Zoom out and evaluate from different perspectives
  2. Understand the context
  3. Identify moments of opportunity and intervention
  4. Co-create new solutions
  5. Create a common understanding
